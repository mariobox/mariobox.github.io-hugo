Reading List

I am an avid reader of business books. These are some books I have read and found useful, listed in no particular order.

Made to Stick: Why Some Ideas Survive and Others Die

Read this book to learn how to make people pay attention, remember, and act on your ideas. Learn to identify when others are trying to manipulate you, too!

Rework

This book grabs almost every piece of business conventional wisdom and shatters it. Reading it is an eye-opening experience. It will force you to reconsider everything you thought was true about business and make you work in a more practical way. Some say this book applies only to small businesses and start-ups, but I find it applicable to business in general, no matter the size of the company. One of the best business books I've ever read.

Work the System: The Simple Mechanics of Making More and Working Less

Ground breaking book about the importance of implementing systems in your business and personal life. Well written, entertaining, rooted in a deeply personal story, the book deals not only with the why but also with the how of developing systems. I wrote a more detailed review here.

I Will Teach You To Be Rich

Quirky title but great primer on personal finance. Everything that should be taught about personal finance in high school but isn't. Ideal for the younger crowd just starting out or for older folk that were never properly introduced to saving and investing. Plenty of tactical advice written in a style that makes an otherwise dry topic fun.

The Personal MBA: Master the Art of Business

Outstanding. I was completely blown away by this book. It starts as a compelling case against spending a small fortune attending business school, and offering an alternative path: anybody can get a quality business education without mortgaging our lives by reading the right books and applying the concepts in real life. It then lays out a comprehensive overview of every single business fundamental, concept and function. To complement the book, the author has curated a list of 99 must-read business books so the reader can delve deeper into the different business subjects. Make sure to check the book's website, where you will find concise outlines of each of the chapters.

If You Can: How Millennials Can Get Rich Slowly

Great primer on how to start investing to build wealth. Very short book (the printed edition is only 27 pages), full of good advice and great recommendations for further reading. The best $0.99 you will ever spend.

I am Zlatan: My Story On and Off the Field

The honest and entertaining biography of Zlatan Ibrahimovic, one of soccer’s greatest (and most misunderstood) stars. An inspiring story about an immigrant kid from the ghetto who managed to make it big while being true to himself, through a combination of talent, hard work, persistence and a winner’s attitude. Loved this book!

Think and Grow Rich

The original “The Secret”. A motivational book based on the notion that the way you think determines your level of success.

Many modern day gurus have appropriated the ideas and concepts originally developed in this book (not always giving proper credit).

The book has many good points and actionable ideas, but at the same time there’s a lot of new-agey kind of stuff (surprising for a book that’s almost 80 years old).

Worth a read, though. The best chapters, in my opinion: Chapter 15 (on Fear), Chapter 5 (on Specialized Knowledge-where he introduces the popular MasterMind principle), Chapter 6 (on Immagination) and Chapter 7 (on Organized Planning).

Choose Yourself!

This is James Altucher’s latest book, and in my opinion his best. This book will help you make sense of the new realities of the world of work.

The author explains how an era dominated by old paradigms like job security, benefits, stability, and college credentials as predictors of success is coming to an end and giving way to the age of the artist/entrepreneur.

The book also gives us a blueprint we can follow to succeed in these new, uncharted waters: to take care of our mental, physical, emotional and spiritual needs, and train ourselves to generate and sell ideas.

You can either love or hate James Altucher but, anyway, this is book is a must-read to better understand why the old recipees for success no longer work.

Remote

Not as good as their previous book Rework, Jason Fried and David Heinemeier Hansson nevertheless make a good case for remote working, and demonstrate that not embracing it has little to do with technology an more with fear of losing control.

While the book’s content could probably be fit for a long essay or article rather than a whole book it is still an interesting read, especially the tips to manage and motivate a remote work force.

Ikigai

Sebastian Marshall is a bright, self-taught twentysomething living in Asia, who is determined to leave his mark in the strategy consulting world. Drawing heavily from history and the careful study of classic and current business books, he offers insightful advice on strategy, productivity and business in general.

The book was put together in a week (mostly from blog content) as a dare to an old-school publisher who had offered to publish it but had been dragging the project for months. As a result, some passages may sound repetitive and there are several grammatical and spelling errors, but don’t get fooled: there is gold on those pages if you just can see beyond the surface.

Presentation Zen

This is an extremely well written book that goes beyond the traditional idea of a business presentation (Powerpoint slide after Powerpoint slide packed with tables and bullet points) and teaches you how to communicate effectively by embracing a minimalist approach. Just as a logo is not a brand, the slides are not the presentation. They exist only to help you tell a story more effectively. This is probably the only business book that can double up as coffee table book! The illustrations and imagery of Japanese culture that the author uses to support his points are simply gorgeous, so I recomment getting the paper book, not the Kindle version.

How to Win at the Sport of Business

Mark Cuban took a collection of blog posts, gave them flow and direction and bundled it all up in an eBook, where he shares some of his most valuable business tenets as well as inspiring and entertaining stories from his beginnings as a salesman and an entrepreneur. Short, easy to read and full of good advice.

Read (without review)
